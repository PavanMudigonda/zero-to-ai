# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of this AI/ML learning repository pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy learning community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community:

* **Being welcoming to learners at all levels** - Everyone starts somewhere. Remember your first steps in AI/ML.
* **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** - Learning can be challenging; be patient and supportive.
* **Providing constructive feedback** - Explain *why* something could be improved and *how* to improve it.
* **Accepting constructive criticism gracefully** - We're all here to learn and grow.
* **Focusing on what's best for the community** - Prioritize collective learning over individual ego.
* **Showing appreciation** - Thank contributors and acknowledge their efforts.
* **Asking questions** - No question is too basic. Curiosity drives learning.
* **Sharing knowledge generously** - Help others understand complex concepts.
* **Admitting mistakes** - Errors are learning opportunities for everyone.

### Educational Best Practices

In the context of this learning repository:

* **Explain concepts thoroughly** - Prefer clarity over brevity in educational content.
* **Provide context** - Help learners understand *why* something matters, not just *how* it works.
* **Use beginner-friendly language** - Avoid unnecessary jargon; define technical terms when used.
* **Include practical examples** - Show real-world applications of concepts.
* **Cite sources** - Give credit to papers, tutorials, and resources that informed your work.
* **Test your code** - Ensure all notebooks run successfully before contributing.
* **Update deprecated content** - Keep learning materials current with modern best practices.

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
* **Gatekeeping** - Dismissing questions as "too basic" or telling learners they shouldn't be here
* **Elitism** - Suggesting that certain tools, languages, or approaches are beneath consideration
* **Condescension** - Talking down to learners or making them feel inadequate
* **Plagiarism** - Copying code or content without attribution
* **Spam** - Promoting unrelated products, services, or content
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or educational setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

* GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
* Code reviews and comments
* Notebook content and documentation
* Any public spaces where you represent the project or community

## Reporting

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by:

1. **Opening a private issue** - Mark it as "Conduct Issue" (maintainers only will see it)
2. **Emailing the maintainers** - Contact information available in the repository
3. **Using GitHub's report feature** - For severe violations

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:

* Your contact information
* Names (GitHub usernames) of people involved
* Description of what happened
* Links to relevant content (issues, PRs, comments)
* Any additional context that might help

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We respect the privacy of those who report issues and will only share information on a need-to-know basis for resolution.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from maintainers, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Learning-Focused Guidance

### For Learners

* **Ask questions freely** - There are no "stupid" questions. If something is unclear, ask!
* **Share your struggles** - Others likely have similar challenges. Your question helps everyone.
* **Be patient with yourself** - AI/ML is complex. Progress over perfection.
* **Experiment** - Try things, break things, learn from failures.

### For Contributors

* **Remember the learner's perspective** - What seems obvious to you may be new to others.
* **Provide context and reasoning** - Explain your thought process, not just the solution.
* **Encourage exploration** - Suggest experiments and variations to try.
* **Celebrate progress** - Acknowledge improvements, no matter how small.

### For Reviewers

* **Be specific and actionable** - Instead of "this is wrong," explain what could be improved and why.
* **Praise good practices** - Highlight what was done well, not just what needs fixing.
* **Suggest, don't demand** - "Consider trying X" is better than "You must use X."
* **Link to resources** - Help contributors learn by pointing to relevant documentation or examples.

## Our Learning Values

This repository is built on these principles:

1. **Mastery through Practice** - Deep understanding comes from doing, not just reading.
2. **Progressive Learning** - Build from fundamentals to advanced topics systematically.
3. **Practical Application** - Every concept should connect to real-world use cases.
4. **Modern Best Practices** - Use current tools and techniques (as of December 2025).
5. **Inclusive Education** - Make AI/ML accessible to everyone willing to learn.
